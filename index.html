Ramen

Ramen originated from China and was introduced to Japan in the late 19th century by Chinese immigrants. Initially a simple street food, it evolved in Japan to include various regional variations like Shoyu, Tonkotsu, and Miso ramen. After WWII, Japan's economic boom saw the proliferation of ramen shops and the invention of instant ramen by Momofuku Ando in 1958, making it accessible worldwide. Today, ramen enjoys global popularity with a thriving culture of gourmet creations and festivals.

Egg fried rice

Egg fried rice is believed to have originated in China thousands of years ago. It likely evolved as a way to use leftover rice and ingredients. The dish consists of stir-fried rice with scrambled eggs, vegetables, and often soy sauce. As Chinese culinary traditions spread globally, variations of egg fried rice developed, becoming popular in many countries. Today, it's enjoyed worldwide as a versatile and beloved dish.
